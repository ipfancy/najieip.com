Why We Joined Forces

Innovation is never an isolated act. An engineer makes a breakthrough in the lab — and needs a patent. A startup closes its seed round — and needs an IP framework. A manufacturer discovers a competitor is copying its product — and needs litigation counsel. These scenarios often unfold within the same month, or even the same week, for a single client.

Under the old model, clients would separately engage a patent agency, a law firm, and an IP consultancy. Information was siloed, communication costs were high, and workflows rarely aligned. Today, NaJie Intellectual Property, APNaJie Patent Agency, and MiLi Law Firm announce a deep operational alliance — stitching three capabilities into one seamless thread, delivering support that is always within reach for innovators and legal service seekers alike.

NaJie handles the strategic layer: Should your technology be protected by patents or trade secrets? Should your trademark portfolio expand overseas now, or later? These questions are answered at project inception — before you commit resources in the wrong direction.

APNaJie handles execution: From invention disclosure to the grant certificate, a hundred technical decisions stand in between. Our patent attorneys average over eight years of experience across chemistry, mechanics, electronics, and software. We know what examiners look for, where rejections originate, and how to draft claims that survive invalidation challenges.

MiLi handles both offense and defense: After grant — what if someone infringes? What if you receive a cease-and-desist letter? How should IP clauses in a technology collaboration agreement be drafted so you do not give away more than you intend? MiLi's litigators come from the courtroom, not the textbook. Their answers are informed by actual disputes, not hypothetical scenarios.
